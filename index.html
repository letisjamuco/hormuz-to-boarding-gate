CH·02 · HORMUZ vs THE WORLD

Why does Hormuz matter more than any other chokepoint?

There are 28 maritime chokepoints tracked by IMF PortWatch. Hormuz is not always the busiest by total ships — but when you look at tanker traffic, it stands apart. Click a chokepoint on the map or a bar in the ranking — they're linked.

CH·03 · WHAT KIND OF SHIPS?

Not all ships carry the same meaning.

Tankers dominate the Hormuz vessel mix — making it primarily an energy chokepoint, not just a shipping lane. Hover over a vessel type to learn more.

Tankers ↗
Ships carrying bulk liquids: crude oil, petroleum products, or gas cargoes.
Containers ↗
Ships carrying standardised container boxes with consumer goods.
Dry bulk ↗
Ships carrying unpackaged solids: grain, ore, coal.
General cargo ↗
Mixed goods not counted as containers, tankers or bulk.
Ro-Ro ↗
Roll-on/roll-off: vehicles drive on/off the ship.
